Light roasts emphasize the coffee's bright acidity, enhancing fruity and citrusy notes. It's the least bitter but can be overshadowed by additives. Ideal for experiencing diverse tasting notes intensely.

 

Medium roasts offer a fuller body and a sweeter, roastier profile. While retaining brightness, they bring darker, richer flavors for a balanced complexity. Perfect for a well-rounded, approachable cup.

 

Dark roasts exhibit a heavy body and deep, nutty taste. Bright notes diminish in favor of dark chocolate tones and a toffee-like sweetness. It leans toward bitterness but pairs well with milk or flavorings. Great for those seeking a robust, balanced latte or a strong, direct coffee experience.

 

French roasts feature a heavy body with very dark, oily beans. They lose subtlety in favor of intense dark flavors and deep, toasty bitterness. They may have a hint of creaminess. Suitable for an incredibly strong and less nuanced coffee experience.
